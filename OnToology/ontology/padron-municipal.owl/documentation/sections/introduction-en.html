Introduction back to ToC

Vocabulary for the representation of the census data that come from the relation of inhabitants files exchanged by the City Councils and the Instituto Nacional de Estadística. This vocabulary has been developed in the context of the project "Plataforma de gobierno abierto, colaborativa e interoperable" (Open Government collaborative and interoperable platform), where the municipalities of Madrid, Zaragoza, Santiago de Compostela and A Coruña are involved. It reuses terms from the [schema.org](http://schema.org/) vocabulary which includes concepts and relationships used for similar purposes. Additionally, it refers to terms from other vocabularies used for the representation of concepts in domains such as administrative divisions, postal address, and street. More specifically, it reuses the [schema.org](http://schema.org/) vocabulary to represent the inhabitants. It also uses the [territory](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/sector-publico/territorio#) vocabulary to describe the territorial entities in Spain, such as district, collective entity, neighborhood, etc., where the inhabitant resides. In addition, the [postal-address](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/direccion-postal#) vocabulary is also reused to describe the residence address of the inhabitant. Finally, it also reuses, by means of the properties defined in the [postal-address](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/direccion-postal#) vocabulary, the [callejero]( http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/callejero#) vocabulary to represent details of the road where the inhabitant's home is located.
